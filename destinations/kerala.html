Why visit Kerala

Kerala is known as “God’s Own Country” for a reason: palm-fringed backwaters, tea gardens in the hills, quiet beaches and a slower rhythm than many other parts of India. It works well for relaxed trips with simple routes and plenty of nature.

Travelers usually combine Kochi, the Alleppey backwaters and a hill station like Munnar. You can add beaches such as Varkala or Kovalam, or wildlife parks like Periyar for a longer trip with variety in landscapes and experiences.

Good for

  • Couple and family trips
  • Slow travel and nature stays
  • Ayurveda, food and photography

Highlights & must-see

Alleppey & Kumarakom backwaters

Canals, lagoons and lakes with houseboats and village life by the water.

Munnar tea hills

Layered tea estates, cool weather and viewpoints above the clouds.

Fort Kochi

Colonial lanes, churches, Chinese fishing nets and a compact old town feel.

Varkala & Kovalam beaches

Cliffside cafes, sunsets and calm, palm-backed stretches of sand.

Things to do in Kerala

Backwater cruise or stay

Spend a day or night on a houseboat, or stay in a homestay by the water.

  • Time: 1–2 days
  • Tip: For quieter vibes, choose smaller canals over main routes.

Hill station time in Munnar

Walk through tea gardens, visit viewpoints and enjoy cooler air.

  • Time: 2–3 days
  • Tip: Start early for viewpoints before clouds roll in.

Explore Fort Kochi & Mattancherry

Street art, cafes, churches, synagogue and waterfront sunset views.

  • Time: 1 full day
  • Tip: Walkable area; keep half day just for wandering and cafes.

4-day Kerala highlights

Short trip

  • Day 1: Kochi arrival, Fort Kochi walk
  • Day 2: Drive to Alleppey, backwaters cruise or stay
  • Day 3: Backwaters morning, return to Kochi
  • Day 4: Kochi city and departure

6–7 day classic Kerala

Balanced route

  • Days 1–2: Kochi and Fort Kochi
  • Days 3–4: Munnar tea gardens and viewpoints
  • Days 5–6: Alleppey backwaters (houseboat or homestay)
  • Day 7: Return to Kochi or continue to beach

Where to stay in Kerala

Fort Kochi / Kochi city

Good base for arrivals and departures, with heritage stays and cafes.

Best for first and last nights of the trip.

Munnar or other hill stations

Cooler climate, tea views and quieter nights away from the coast.

Works well mid-trip to slow down.

Backwaters & beaches

Houseboats or waterfront homestays in Alleppey, plus beach stays at Varkala/Kovalam.

Ideal for off-screen, low-activity days.

Local tips for Kerala

Simple habits that help with weather, transport and food.

  • Monsoon months (Jun–Sep) can bring heavy rain; keep buffer time in plans.
  • Book houseboats with clear hygiene and safety reviews, not only by low price.
  • Use life jackets on boats and follow staff instructions on water.
  • Food is often coconut-based and can be spicy; ask for medium spice if needed.
