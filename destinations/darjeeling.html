Why visit Darjeeling

Darjeeling sits on ridges above the plains with tea gardens, colonial-era charm and broad Himalayan panoramas. Its compact town and nearby hill villages make it easy to combine short hikes, tea-estate visits and cultural stops.

The UNESCO-listed Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (toy train) and Tiger Hill sunrise over Kanchenjunga are signature experiences. Expect cool mornings, quick weather changes and strong tea.

Good for

  • Photography and sunrise viewpoints
  • Tea-lovers and relaxed hill walks
  • Short cultural side-trips into Sikkim or nearby villages

Things to do in Darjeeling

Top 3

1. Watch sunrise at Tiger Hill

Pre-dawn drive or early morning trek to Tiger Hill for an unforgettable Kanchenjunga sunrise.

  • Time: Early morning (start before dawn)
  • Tip: Leave early; winter mornings are very cold.

2. Ride the Darjeeling toy train

Historic narrow-gauge ride between New Jalpaiguri / Ghum and Darjeeling. Iconic engineering and views.

  • Time: 1–3 hours depending on segment
  • Tip: Book in advance and aim for window seats.

3. Visit a tea estate

Guided estate tours explain plucking, processing and tasting of Darjeeling tea.

  • Time: 1–2 hours
  • Tip: Combine with a short walk through plantation lanes.

Local tips for Darjeeling

Practical habits for cold mornings, transport and views.

  • Book Tiger Hill or toy-train early. Visibility is weather-dependent.
  • Carry warm layers and a windproof jacket; mornings and evenings are chilly.
  • Altitude is modest but roads are steep; avoid tight schedules if prone to motion sickness.
  • Respect local monasteries. Ask before photographing inside prayer halls.
  • Cash can be useful in small shops and tea garden homestays.
