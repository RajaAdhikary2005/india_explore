Misty hills, endless waterfalls, living root bridges, and one of the wettest places on Earth.

Why visit Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji, locally known as Sohra, is famous for its rainfall, green cliffs, and breathtaking waterfall canyons. Visitors come to experience living root bridges, deep forests, and cool mountain air.

It is an ideal destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and travelers seeking a peaceful and scenic escape.

Good for

  • Waterfall lovers
  • Trekking & nature walks
  • Photography & scenic drives

Top highlights

Nohkalikai Waterfall

One of India’s tallest waterfalls, famous for its emerald pool and dramatic cliffs.

Double Decker Living Root Bridge

Iconic bio-engineered bridge requiring a long but rewarding trek.

Mawsmai Caves

Short limestone cave system with narrow passages and unique rock formations.

Things to do in Cherrapunji

Trek to Nongriat village

A challenging descent into the valley leading to the famous root bridges.

  • Time: 4–6 hours
  • Tip: Start early and carry water.

Visit Seven Sisters Waterfall

Long vertical falls especially stunning during the monsoon.

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Best view: Afternoon.

Local tips for Cherrapunji

Simple travel guidance useful for most visitors.

  • Weather changes fast — carry a light rain jacket.
  • Footpaths can be slippery. Wear good trekking shoes.
  • Mobile network is weak in valleys — download maps offline.
