Pangong Lake

A high-altitude, crystal-blue lake surrounded by rugged mountains, famous for its surreal colors.

Why visit Pangong Lake

Pangong Lake is one of India’s most iconic natural wonders. Famous for its shifting shades of blue, the lake stretches across India and into Tibet, offering breathtaking scenery and peaceful silence.

The high altitude, open skies, and dramatic landscapes make it a dream location for photographers, adventure travelers, and anyone seeking raw Himalayan beauty.

Good for

  • Photography lovers
  • Adventure & road-trip travelers
  • Camping under the stars

Top highlights

Shifting Blue Waters

The lake changes color from turquoise to deep blue throughout the day.

Himalayan Mountain Views

Surrounded by rugged peaks and vast open landscapes unique to Ladakh.

Camping at Spangmik

Experience peaceful nights with crystal-clear skies filled with stars.

Things to do in Pangong

Lakeside Photography

Pangong is one of India's most photographed natural landscapes.

  • Best time: Morning & evening
  • Tip: Bring a polarizing filter for better photos.

Camping Experience

Stay in lakefront camps at Spangmik, Man, or Merak.

  • Temperature: Nights can be very cold
  • Tip: Carry warm layers even in summer.

Ideal plans if you're visiting from Leh.

1-day quick trip

From Leh to Pangong and back

  • Drive via Chang La Pass
  • Spend time at Spangmik viewpoint
  • Return before night

2-day relaxed plan

Best experience

  • Day 1: Leh → Pangong, camping at Spangmik
  • Day 2: Sunrise at lake + return to Leh

Where to stay near Pangong

Basic tent camps

Simple tents with beds and blankets close to the lake.

Good for one night if you are okay with cold and basic facilities.

Deluxe camps & cottages

Better bedding, attached washrooms and slightly more insulation from the cold.

Best choice for most travelers.

Stay in Leh, day trip to Pangong

Base yourself in Leh hotels and visit Pangong on a long day trip.

More comfort, but less time beside the lake.

Pangong in pictures

Local tips for Pangong

Important advice due to the high-altitude environment.

  • Acclimatize in Leh for 24–48 hours before visiting.
  • Carry thermals — nights are extremely cold even in summer.
  • Network is very limited. Inform family before leaving Leh.
