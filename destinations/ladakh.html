Why visit Ladakh

Ladakh is a landscape of wide skies, stark mountains and calm Buddhist monasteries. It offers unique high-altitude experiences: blue lakes (Pangong, Tso Moriri), desert vistas and local culture.

Travelers come for road trips, trekking, photography, and to explore remote valleys like Nubra and Zanskar. Expect strong sunlight, cold nights and thin air. Acclimatise in Leh before heading higher.

Good for

  • Road-trippers and motorbike tours
  • Photographers and landscape lovers
  • Short treks and cultural visits

Highlights & must-see

Pangong Tso

High-altitude lake famed for its changing colors and remote shoreline.

Nubra Valley & Diskit

Sandy dunes, double-humped Bactrian camels and mountain vistas.

Thiksey & Hemis Monasteries

Large gompas with rich festivals, murals and cultural significance.

Sham Valley & Tso Moriri

Remote quiet lakes and traditional nomadic settlements.

Things to do in Ladakh

Drive the Leh–Manali or Leh–Srinagar highway

Epic high-altitude road trips crossing multiple passes and dramatic landscapes.

  • Time: Multi-day, plan for acclimatisation
  • Tip: Check permits and road conditions seasonally.

Visit Pangong Tso & Tso Moriri

Lake visits with sunrise/sunset light and camping options.

  • Time: Half-day to full-day visits
  • Tip: Carry warm layers and avoid long exposure without acclimatisation.

Trek short routes

Day treks around Markha, Stok or Lamayuru for varied terrain and views.

  • Time: 4–8 hours (day treks)
  • Tip: Hire local guides for safety and route info.

itineraries for Ladakh

Adjust pace to allow for acclimatisation in Leh.

3-day: Leh + Pangong

Quick trip

  • Day 1: Acclimatise in Leh, local monasteries.
  • Day 2: Drive to Pangong, evening by the lake.
  • Day 3: Return to Leh via Chang La.

5–6 day: Leh, Nubra, Pangong

Classic circuit

  • Day 1: Leh local sights
  • Day 2: Leh → Nubra (Hunder)
  • Day 3: Explore Nubra
  • Day 4: Nubra → Pangong via Shyok
  • Day 5: Pangong → Leh

7+ day: Extended

Includes Tso Moriri & remote valleys

  • Combine Pangong, Tso Moriri, Sham Valley and short treks

Where to stay

Leh: Guesthouses & hotels

Comfortable hotels and guesthouses with services and acclimatisation support.

Best base for exploring Ladakh.

Camps by Pangong / Nubra

Lakefront tents and eco-camps. Expect basic to deluxe options depending on operator.

Book in advance during peak season.

Homestays in villages

Local stays offering cultural interaction and simple comforts.

Good for longer stays and cultural experiences.

Local tips for Ladakh

Essentials for high-altitude travel and comfort.

  • Acclimatise: stay in Leh 24–48 hours before higher altitudes.
  • Hydrate and avoid overexertion during first days.
  • Carry sunscreen, sunglasses and warm layers for nights.
  • Respect local customs at monasteries; photography rules vary.
